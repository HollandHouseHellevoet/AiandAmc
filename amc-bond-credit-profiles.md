## Sector Context

### Macro Credit Environment (2025–2026)

- **Moody's sector outlook:** Stable for 2026, but downgrade-to-upgrade ratio was 2.0:1 in 2024 before moderating to 0.6:1 in 2025. Multi-notch downgrades expected to continue.
- **Fitch median operating margin forecast:** 1%–2% for 2026, below pre-pandemic 2.3% (2019). Sector described as "trifurcated" — top 20% growing, middle 65% stagnating, bottom 15% deteriorating.
- **Drug cost inflation:** +15.2% YoY; supply expenses +13.2%; purchased services +11.7%.
- **Site-neutral payment reform:** CMS finalized 40% OPPS rate for drug administration in off-campus HOPDs, cutting ~$290M in CY2026. Eleven states pursuing facility fee legislation.
- **NIH indirect cost cap:** February 2025 policy capped indirect costs at 15% (down from avg 58%). Court-enjoined but under appeal. If implemented, projected $5.24B annual funding shortfall for research institutions. States with highest exposure: California, New York, Pennsylvania.

### Structural Risk Factors for AMC Debt

1. **Credential scarcity erosion** — AI-augmented diagnostics, robot-assisted surgery, simulation-based training, and remote proctoring decouple physician skill acquisition from physical volume at a single institution. The competitive moat that justified $2B+ surgical tower construction is narrowing.

2. **NIH indirect cost vulnerability** — Institutions modeled for 50–70% recovery rates face margin impairment if the 15% cap survives legal challenge or is partially implemented through legislative compromise.

3. **Site-neutral payment compression** — Facility fee revenue that historically cross-subsidized research and training is under active federal and state legislative attack.

4. **Political protection paradox** — The institutions most exposed sit at the intersection of academic tenure, hospital lobbying, federal grant dependency, and municipal bond markets. Each constituency has structural incentives to suppress acknowledgment of deteriorating fundamentals.

## Data Limitations and Flags

- **DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio):** Not publicly available for any of the 25 institutions in web-accessible sources. DSCR is typically disclosed only in official statements filed on EMMA or in continuing disclosure documents behind paywalls. A complete DSCR analysis would require direct EMMA filing review or Bloomberg Terminal access.

- **S&P Ratings:** S&P Global Ratings publishes behind a paywall for most institutions. Only 8 of 25 institutions had publicly confirmable S&P ratings. Remaining marked "Not Found."

- **Debt Figures:** 7 of 25 institutions had no publicly available debt figures in web-accessible sources. Figures marked [UNVERIFIED] are derived from older filings or estimated from rating action reports rather than audited financials.

- **University vs. Hospital Ratings:** Several institutions (U of Michigan, Emory, UCSF) issue debt at the university level rather than the hospital obligated group level. University-level ratings may not reflect hospital-specific credit risk.

- **Operating Margin Data:** Operating margins are sourced from Becker's Hospital Review, Fierce Healthcare, and institutional press releases. Not all institutions disclose operating margin publicly.
